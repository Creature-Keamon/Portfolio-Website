Spam Detection AI

In year 13, for my final Secondary School project, I decided to build my own AI model. I intitially was going to build a very basic smart home assistant, comparing to the likes of Siri, Google Assistant and Alexa. As this was my first forte into developing Artificial Intelligence models, I decided that I would reduce my scope to make it more managable and educational without bogging me down with having to add all of these smart home features.

So I found some fantastic educational content on how Artificial Intelligence models work under the hood, as well as how to assemble one in Python using TensorFlow and Keras. I decided to build an AI model that can detect Spam messages, I chose this because I found a couple of fantastic datasets that had clearly and concisely compiled their data, so I could easily extract the data and use it in my model.

After many tests and tweaks, I managed to create a working Artificial Intelligence model. It was very confident with it's results, almost always picking either 0 (meaning the text is legitmate) and 1 (meaning the text is spam), here is an example of it's accurate results.
