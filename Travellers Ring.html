The Traveller's Ring

The Traveller's Ring is a short film created for the New Zealand 2024 48 Hour Film Competition, and was my first ever film that I have created seriously, although "serious" is an overstatement. It follows four guys and their mishaps while playing Dungeons and Dragons, where it frequently switches between them in the real world and their game world.

My first venture into creating films not on a phone camera, The Traveller's Ring was an experience, where technical issues were frequent and detrimental to the outcome of the film. My job was the boom arm operator, as well as chief editor and did most of the post production. The boom arm had multiple issues, causing it to lose a lot the audio that we so painstakingly recorded on set, or for the audio to be so overpowered by an incredible amount of white noise. DaVinci Resolve's white noise remover helped but there is only so much it can do.

Since this was a film that had to be made in the span of 48 hours, we were on a tight time pressure meaning we could not fix some of the issues that arose like the aforementioned white noise issue, as well as some weird cuts between shots that kill the immersion. The final output could be better but we are proud of what we achieved.
